About

Pocket DJ is likely the smallest standalone DJ system ever built. No laptop, no external computer, no compromises. Mount a USB drive, browse your tracks on a 2" screen, and mix — all from something that fits in your back pocket.

The magic is in the standalone architecture. The Orange Pi Zero is a tiny single-board computer running Linux, giving full access to the libraries needed to mount, read, and play USB drive content with a custom DSP mixing engine. Consumer DJ units that do this cost $2,000+ and are massive. Lower-priced portable units still need a laptop connection. Pocket DJ needs neither.

Audio goes out over Bluetooth to any speaker or hardwired through the onboard PCM DAC. The main build runs a full GUI with an onboard SLM that can DJ for you. Both versions run a lightweight mixing engine built from scratch with DSP.

Every standalone DJ unit on the consumer market is bigger and more expensive. This one fits in your pocket.
